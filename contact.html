Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Bhutan?

The best time to visit Bhutan is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. Spring offers beautiful rhododendron blooms, while autumn provides excellent views of the Himalayas and is when most festivals take place.

Do I need a visa to visit Bhutan?

Yes, all foreign visitors (except citizens of India, Bangladesh, and Maldives) require a visa to enter Bhutan. The visa must be arranged through a licensed Bhutanese tour operator like Balungbi Tours and Travels. We handle the entire visa application process for our clients as part of our tour packages.

What is included in your tour packages?

Our standard tour packages include accommodation in 3-star hotels (with upgrades available), all meals, licensed English-speaking guide, private transportation, entry fees to monuments and museums, and the government sustainable development fee. International flights and travel insurance are not included.

How difficult are the treks in Bhutan?

Bhutan offers treks for all levels of fitness. We have easy treks suitable for beginners (like the Druk Path Trek) as well as challenging high-altitude treks (like the Snowman Trek). Our team will help you choose a trek that matches your fitness level and provide all necessary support including guides, porters, and equipment.

What is your cancellation policy?

For cancellations made more than 60 days before departure, we offer a full refund minus a 10% administrative fee. Between 30-60 days, we refund 50% of the payment. Less than 30 days before departure, no refund is possible. We strongly recommend purchasing travel insurance to cover unexpected cancellations.
